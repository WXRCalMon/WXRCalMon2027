# The 2027 Weather Radar and Calibration Workshop
This workshop continues a series previously hosted by DWD (2017, 2019), Météo-France (2021), the UK Met Office (2023), and again DWD (2025).

### Scope
Exchange of experiences and future plans on methods and tools for (DualPol) Weather Radar—operation, monitoring, and calibration. The aim is for the community to:
* Share experiences with current approaches to radar calibration and monitoring
* Examine challenges facing radar networks and strategies for mitigation.
* Discuss emerging technologies and techniques to support national meteorological services.
* Promote international best practices.

Manufacturers and service providers are also welcome to participate. We are particularly interested in detailed discussions on calibration and monitoring procedures for dual-polarization weather radars, especially SSPA radars. Please note: Sales presentations will not be accepted.

We encourage the participation of engineers and scientists who have hands-on experience in running operational radars.

### Topics
Contributions welcome in:
1. Experiences with Monitoring of Radar Systems
    * Absolute calibration, monitoring, and calibration adjustments – Best practices, challenges, and advancements.
    * Interference detection and mitigation – Including sources such as other transmitters, communication networks, and natural interference.
    * Wind turbine clutter – Assessment, mitigation strategies, and case studies. 
    * Phase monitoring & adjustment – Approaches for ensuring long-term stability.
    * Emerging monitoring methods – AI-based techniques, automation, and innovative methodologies.
    * Live demonstration of monitoring systems – Showcasing real-time applications, tools, and dashboards.
2. SSPA- Systems
    * Data quality and operational experience – Lessons learned from deployments and use, signal processing methods
    * Monitoring techniques – Methods for ensuring system performance and reliability.
    * Frequency protection strategies – Addressing spectrum allocation challenges and interference risks
3. Engineering Challenges & Network Planning
    * Network renewal and extension – Strategies for expanding and upgrading radar networks.
    * System upgrades and life-cycle challenges – Midlife upgrades, component obsolescence, and modernization efforts.
    * Infrastructure challenges – Radome, Power supply, site selection, and long-term maintenance.
    * Optimization of network coverage and redundancy – Improving reliability and efficiency.
    * Monitoring TR-limiters – Experiences with (isotope-free) TR-limiters and performance tracking.
    * Transmitter issues and best practices – Longevity, failure modes, maintenance strategies, and technological improvements.
    * New technologies and approaches – Innovations shaping the future of weather radar networks.
    * Data formats – Handling, standardizing, and integrating radar moments and I/Q-data across networks.
